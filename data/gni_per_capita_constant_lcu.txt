GNI per capita is gross national income divided by midyear population. 
GNI (formerly GNP) is the sum of value added by all resident producers 
plus any product taxes (less subsidies) not included in the valuation of 
output plus net receipts of primary income (compensation of employees and property income) from abroad. 
Data are in constant local currency.

Source: World Bank national accounts data, and OECD National Accounts data files.
